Hi!. I’m Sarah Lee, a Psychology student at the University of California, Riverside, with a deep passion for pediatric nursing and a lifelong goal of working in healthcare to support children and families, especially those in low-income and underserved communities. My interest in nursing goes beyond clinical care. It’s rooted in a desire to create a more equitable healthcare system where every child, regardless of background or income, has access to compassionate, quality care.

Growing up in a family of immigrants, I’ve seen firsthand how difficult it can be to navigate healthcare systems that aren’t built for everyone. Language barriers, lack of insurance, and cultural misunderstandings often prevent families from receiving the care they need. These experiences have shaped my mission to become a pediatric nurse who not only treats illness but also advocates for those who are often overlooked by the system. I believe that children deserve more than just a diagnosis. They deserve someone who listens, understands, and cares deeply about their long-term well-being.

I also hope to contribute to health education and outreach efforts, helping parents better understand how to care for their children and navigate complex systems. Eventually, I’d like to become a voice for healthcare reform, using my background and lived experiences to push for changes in how we serve immigrant and low-income families.
